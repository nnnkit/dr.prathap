what is

Chiropractic ?

Chiropractic is a science, an art, and a philosophy that allows the body to function better by reducing interference on the nervous system. Using "Chiropractic Adjustments" (technique) to eliminate "Subluxations" (restricted vertebral segments) we are able to allow "Innate Intelligence" to help our bodies heal naturally.Innate Intelligence refers to the body's natural ability to heal itself. Our body is constantly replacing old, dead cells with new ones, without any extra help - it does this on its own! For example, when you cut yourself, blood comes out, then clots, then scabs over. You did not need to take medicine or get surgery to correct this! Chiropractic is a natural, conservative form of healthcare that neither introduces harmful chemicals to the body (medicines), nor does it open the body up to infections (surgeries). In fact, having a properly functioning nervous system improves your body's ability to fight illness. The brain sends signals to your body through the spinal cord. From the spinal cord, pairs of nerve roots exit to form major nerves, which go on to power all of the body's functions. Getting a Chiropractic Adjustment literally "turns the power on!"

about

Doctor

Dr. Prathap Addageethala, or "Dr. Prat" as many of his patients call him, is originally from Toronto, Canada. He completed his Doctorate of Chiropractic in 2012 at New York Chiropractic College, where he excelled as a student and leader.

In clinical practice, Dr. Prathap has established himself by working with multidisciplinary teams in large markets. He has successfully completed clinical rounds in long term care hospitals, war veterans administrations and multidisciplinary clinics in the US and Canada, alongside well-renowned clinicians. He is an expert in soft tissue treatment and certified in Nimmo Technique. His accolades include being honored as a guest speaker at many prestigious institutions around India.

He currently resides and practices in Bangalore, India, as a consulting physician to many clinics. Dr. Prathap Addageethala has an active web presence and currently contributes to his own blog which you can visit HERE (link opens in a new window).
